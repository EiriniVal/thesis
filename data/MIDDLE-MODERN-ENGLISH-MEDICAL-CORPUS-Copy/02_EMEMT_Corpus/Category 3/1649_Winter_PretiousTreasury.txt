[}3. A speciall water for sore Eyes.}] 
 
TAke a Gallon of faire running Water, and put it into a faire Earthen

TAke an old Cock, scald and quarter him and bruise his flesh and bones,

out of the pot, and if it comes to a Jelly, it is enough; if you have not

TAke quick Lime and Saltpeter fined divers times, Tutia Alexandrin_[^ORIG. BLURRED^]

powders in a new Linnen Cloth, and bind them hard. This done, take two 
great Goldsmiths Crosletrees or melting pots, and put the sayd powders 
in them, and set the one upon the other, mouth to mouth, and binde them 
fast with Wyer, and Lusum Sapientiæ, so that it may take no maner of ayr, 
and dry them in the Sun, and when it is dry, the powder wilbe yellow.

cold againe, take it out, and you shall finde your substance of the colour of 
bricke, and it shall be perfect and good. And when you use of it, to make a 
fire, or light a candle, spit upon it, or wet it with a drop of water, and incontinent 
put it to your match, and it will light it and burne. And when 
you will quench it againe, blow on it as you blow out a candle, and it 
will goe out.

[}1. How to make haire to grow.}] 
 
TAke halfe a pound of Aqua Mellis, in the Spring 
time of the yeare, warme a little of it every morning 
when you rise in a Saucer, or such a thing, 
and tye a little Spunge to a fine Boxe Combe, 
and dippe it in the sayd Water; and therewith 
moysten the very Rootes of your Haire in 
combing it, and it will grow long; thick, and 
curled in a very short space. 
 
[}2. How to write Letters either Blacke, Blew, Red, or Greene, 
without Pen or Inke.}] 
 
Take the Ashes of burned Paper, with a little Salle_[^ORIG. BLURRED^] Oyle, and mingle 
it like a thick Oyntment; then take Paper, warme it a little by the fire, 
and anoynt it over with the aforesaid Oyntment, and when you would 
write, lay this anoynted side of your Paper upon a cleane sheete of Paper; 
then take a piece of any strong Wyer, or a little stick smooth and small at 
the end: then write therewith upon the sayd Paper, and it will appeare 
[^p.16^] 
most plainely upon the white Paper. If you would have your Letters blow, 
take Indico, if green, Verdigreese. 
 
[}3. How to write Letters of secr_[^ORIG. BLURRED^]s severall wayes that can be read by [^ORIG. BLURRED^] 
without this instruction following.}] 
 
Take fi_[^ORIG. BLURRED^] Allome, beate it small, and put a reasonable quantity of it 
into a little Water, then write with the sayd Water, the which Writing 
cannot be read, but by steeping your Paper afterwards in faire Water. 
 
You may likewise write with Vinegar or the juce of a Lemon, or of an 
Onyon; then when you would reade the same, you must heate your Paper 
against the fire. 
 
Moreover, if you will anoynt a piece of Paper with Candle-greace, 
and lay it upon another white Paper, then write thereon, next open your 
Paper asunder, and strew a little Powder upon your Paper, and whatsoever 
colour your Powder be of, your Writing will appeare of the same colour. 
 
[}4. How any that in necessity are troubled with Lice, or any other Vermine, 
may destroy them suddainely.}] 
 
Take Lawrell Oyle halfe an ounce, three pennyworth of Quick-Silver, 
mingle them well, and anoynt therewith the places where you have most 
Vermine, and you shall suddainely be cleare from all such things. 
 
[}5. How to make a Horse to have a good hoofe.}] 
 
Take Cowdung and Chimneysoot, mingle them, beate them, and apply 
them to the Horse his hoofes, and binde it on with some course thing, 
and it will doe the effect. 
 
[}6. How to make Wine to be Odoriferous.}] 
 
Stick a Lemon or Orange full of Cloaves, then fasten it to the inside of 
the Barrell in such sort that it touch not the Wine, then stop your Vessell 
very well, that the Odour go not forth, and it will be excellent. 
 
[}7. How to make strong Vinegar.}] 
 
Take Gooseberries and wilde Mulberries when they are greene, dry 
[^p.17^] 
them, beat them into powder, and then make a paste of them with strong 
Vinegar of which you shall make little round Balls, then take your wine, 
and boyl all together, and it will prove excellent good Vineger. 
 
[}8. How to make hayre of any colour to become blacke.}] 
 
Take a little Aqua fortie, put therein a groat or six pence according to 
the quantity of the foresayd water, then set both to dissolve before the 
fire. Lastly, dip a little Spunge in the sayd water, and wet your beard or 
hayre therewith, but touch not the skin. 
 
[}9. How to make a Candle burne in water and endure a long time.}] 
 
Take a Candle, and sticke at the bottome of it as much L_[^ORIG. BLURRED^]d as will make 
it stand upright in a payle of water, and not sinke, and the Candle will endure 
burning therein a long time. 
 
[}10. How to keepe wine from sowring.}] 
 
Tie a piece of very salt Bacon on the inside of your barrell, so as it touch 
not the Wine, in so doing your Wine will never sowre. 
 
[}11. How to ripen Musk_[^ORIG. BLURRED^]s, and such other fruit before their seasons.}] 
 
You must plant them in dry weather, and once every third or fourth 
day water them with warme water, and so they will be ripe before their 
season. 
 
[}12. How to cleare your feet from sweat.}] 
 
Take the filings of Pins, otherwise cal_[^ORIG. BLURRED^]ed Pin-dust, put a little thereof 
into your Shoes or Boots, and it will keepe your feet clear from sweat. 
 
[}13. How to take out spots of oyle or greace.}] 
 
Take the bones of Sheepes feet, burn them almost to ashes, then bruise 
them to powder, and put of it upon your spots, and lay all before the Sun 
when it shines hottest; when the powder becomes blacke, lay on fresh in 
the place till it sucke out the spots, which is done in very short time. 
 
[^p.18^] 
[}14. How to keepe Meat fresh and sweet a long time without salt.}] 
 
Put your Meat in Flower, Meale or Bran, and therein it will keepe fresh 
and sweete a long time, or else cast beaten Coriander seed and Vinegar on 
it, and it will doe the same. 
 
[}15. How to make a Powder that will make good Inke 
in an instant.}] 
 
Take Roman Vitrioll, and Copperas, as much of the one as of the other; 
beate this into powder, mingle it, and put of it into a little water; 
and on the present it will be very good Inke: likewise if you strew of the 
same Powder upon white Paper, and write thereon with a little faire water, 
the Letters will appeare presently black. 
 
[}16. How to cure the Ague quartan, ter_[^ORIG. BLURRED^]an, or quotidian.}] 
 
First take advice of your Doctour; that will be I know to purge, and 
let blood, which indeed is a great preparation for obtaining of health in 
all diseases; yet none can prescribe any certaine rule for this purpose, but 
such a Doctour as partly knowes the constitution of your body, and the 
nature of your disease; therefore if your Ague stick _[^ORIG. BLURRED^]o you after you have 
been under the Doctours hands, take little pieces of Allome, scrape and 
cut them to the fashion of Pills, and let them that are very young, very old, 
or very weake take three of these Allom pills in three spoonfulls of Ann_[^ORIG. BLURRED^]seed 
water; let the _[^ORIG. BLURRED^]ronger sort take five or six, in the same maner; this is 
a remedy whereby I never fayled to cure as many as ever I tooke in hand, 
observing likewise if you can, to use Cardus Benedictus boyled in posset 
drinke for your ordinary drink, especially when you have a mind to sweat. 
 
[}17. How to appease the paine of the Toothach.}] 
 
Take a little Cotton and dip it into effence of Cloves, which you may 
have at the Apothecaries, thrust the ayd dipt Cotton in the hollownesse of 
your tooth, and it will appease the present paine.

[}18. How to make hayre grow where there is none.}] 
 
Take Bees and burne them upon a fire shovell or such a thing, then boyl 
the ashes of the Bees in Sallet Oyl, then anoynt therewith the place where 
you would have hayre to grow, and you shall obtaine your wish in a short 
time. 
 
[}19. How to make a most wholesome and pleasing m_[^ORIG. BLURRED^]sse of Broth for a 
sick body, though his stomack be never so weak for other things.}] 
 
Take the yolks of two new Egges, put them in a little Pipkin for that 
purpose, squeeze therein the Juyce of a Lemon or two, then take a good 
Ladle full of good Broth out of another Pot that boyles, first beate the 
Egges yolkes and Lemon juyce very well together, then put in the said 
Broth, but let it not be too salt by no meanes, then stirre altogether upon 
an easie fire, and at the first boyle thereof take it off and give it the sicke 
body upon a little bread, according to your discretion. 
 
[}20. How to make ones hands and face smooth and faire.}] 
 
Take bitter Almonds, steepe them overnight in water, then pill off 
their skinnes with your fingers, and then beate them very well in a Morter 
with two or three yolkes of Egges; use this Paste to wash your face and 
hands often, and it will make your skinne smooth and faire: to weare 
Dogge leather Gioves is likewise good for that purpose, but especially any 
little delicate Gloves, rub them well with some of the sweetest smelling 
Oyle before a fire, and weare these Gloves under others a while, there 
is nothing in the world better for that purpose.
